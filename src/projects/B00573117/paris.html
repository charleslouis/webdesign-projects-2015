My Top 10 Moments in Paris

 

1. Living at Concorde Opera Paris

In 2010, I came to Paris alone on a whim, without knowing anyone or speaking any French. I was able to receive from room and board at the Concorde Opéra Paris. I was living on the top floor with the hotel’s interns who came from all over the world, such as France, China, Ireland, and the Netherlands, among others. And then there was me—the lone American who did not speak a lick of French being thrown into the wild bunch. There were times when we would all snuggle tightly on a bed and watch movies with French subtitles, and there were times where we would be next to each other, side by side, with our laptops and communicate the only way we could—through Google Translate! But for all the good times shared, there were also an equally number of bad times. In retrospect, I remembered why travel is so important: for little moments of perfection like this.

 

2. Meeting Maxime Simoëns

In 2011, I was able to meet with an up-and-comer and newly-minted Haute Couture designer Maxime Simoëns. I went to Maxime’s atelier for a fitting and photo shoot for Alexandra, and the designer, himself, made an unannounced appearance. He was surprisingly charming, modest, and young! In fact, we’re the same age. Alex tried on many of the dresses from his latest collection, and he gave her carte blanche on choosing any dress she desired. We spent 2-3 hours with him that day and he and his whole team are the most wonderful host.

 

3. Studying at La Sorbonne

For one of my majors at UGA, I needed two years of foreign language to fulfill my degree requirements. As an incorrigible procrastinator, I had been putting them off as they would interfered with pretty much any other courses I wanted. During my last semester of college, I was three language courses shy of graduating. In other words, The only way I would graduate on time was if I took the courses in another country. So, I dropped everything immediately and headed straight to Paris.

The courses were very demanding and the professors were strict. I am constantly amazed at the amount of homework given to us daily. And with the days leading up to the final exam, you can often sense the fear, anxiety, and stress throughout campus. My French improved un peu with each passing semester, and I was able to learn the structural significance of the language and the cultural aspects of French history and politics. The best part about studying at la Sorbonne is that all the students are foreign and that makes you feel less out of place.

5. Attending the Elie Saab Fashion Show during Paris Fashion Week

“Elegance is a statment, an attitude. Elegant women are women of character with confidence.” -Elie Saab

 

6. Living with a French Family

When you're a foreigner in a country, it's a blessing to find people who want to help you in any way possible. I met this family years ago, and they have always treated me as part of their own. We go to the mall together, eat Christmas dinners together, and enjoy family game nights together. It's comforting to have a family in another part of the world.

 

7. Meeting Rocky Balboa

“Life's not about how hard of a hit you can give... it's about how many you can take, and still keep moving forward.” -Rocky Balboa

 

8. Louboutins from Louboutin!

At my former job, we had a partnership with Christian Louboutin for an event and my coworkers and I were each gifted a pair of our own Louboutins. We were ecstatic! The excitement was akin to kids waking up on Christmas morning. I was able to pick any pair of Louboutins which would be custom-made to my liking. After days of deciding, I finally chose the Vampanodo satin bow sandals. The price tag was more than twice my rent.

 

9. Discovering French Food

“When is Paris not just Paris? When it’s a window onto Dakar, Senegal; or Morocco; or Mumbai, India; or Moscow. The city successfully markets itself as a playground in which to sample France’s vast traditional cultural and gastronomic output, from wines to cheeses to literature and fashion. But that’s only half of France’s heritage. So while tourists often flock to the City of Lights to see famous French sites — Notre Dame, the Louvre and, of course, the Eiffel Tower — that are clustered, for the most part, in the center of the city, they may miss a global metropolis that offers a sampling of many different cultures.” -Julien Bourgeois for The New York Times

 

10. Le Bal

Free stays in hotels, free Louboutins, meeting fashion designers, partying with photographers from Teen Vogue, chatting with celebrities, and having cocktails at the US Ambassador to France's residence are some of the perks of working here. Then, there are days when there's just yelling and screaming, silent treatments, and profanity-laced conversations. But the best part of working here was hanging out with the coolest co-workers.
